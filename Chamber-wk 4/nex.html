Our Sahuarita

Sahuarita is a town that lies adjacent to the community of Green Valley and is 15 miles south of Tucson, AZ. Sahuarita itself lies 50 miles north of the US-Mexico Border. Although it was officially recognized as a town in 1994, Saahuarita has been home to many for thousands of years.

A Multicultural Community

In 2020, over 30% of Black Forest's population reported as non-Caucasian. Black Forest is a melting pot of cultures. From our early European and American pioneers interacting with Native Americans, to the influx of Dutch immigrants in the 1890's,and carrying into the modern-day, we celebrate our cultural heritage and differences.

A Community that Values Education

Black Forest University consistently ranks in the top 50 highest-rated universities in the American Midwest. Education has always been a core value of Black Forest, and a full 68% of Black Forest adults having some form of higher education.

A Community of Families

Marriage and families have always been important among Colorado communities, and Black Forest is no different. Our local library and community center both host a number of events for couples and families, and we also have a number of festivals and other family-friendly events.

The Colorado Kid was commissioned in 1907 to commemorate Black Forest's rich cowboy history, including famous names such as Butch Cassidy and the Sundance Kid who once called Black Forest home.

Black Forest has a thriving downtown which is home to many of Colorado's Fortune 500 companies.

Come join us on every May 13th for Black Forest Days, an annual celebration commemorating Black Forest's rich history. There will be food, games, and appearances from celebrities and musicians.

Visit Black Forest's original City Center, a historic part of town that has been in use since the town's founding in 1853. One popular site is the Ursine Academy, which was founded in 1872 to serve the learning needs of the community's youth.

Black Forest is home to a wide range of beautiful parks, including the Jamon Iberico de Bellota Park in upper Black Forest. With an average population growth of 2%, Black Forest has plenty of resources catering to the needs of our young citizens.
