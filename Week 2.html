White Space & Clean Design

  • google.com

    • In my first section, I would like to discuss white-space and clean design. With any web page, too much clutter would drive anyone crazy. White space or "negative space" is a principal of web design which is empty space around the content and functional elements of a page. By limiting the content and functional elements the page can in a way breathe and help the browsing parties be focused on the necessary elements in the pages design. I chose Google as my example. The main function of Google is to make an internet search. The page maintains a simple design with the logo being large and centralized. Then below it is placed the search bar. This simple and clean design helps focus the browsers (people) towards the intended use of the page.

    Hick's Law

  • amazon.com

    • Hicks Law clearly stated is that, the more choices you present your user with the longer they will take to make a desicion. If we think about the old TV remotes and all the buttons we had, we would get frustrated trying to do anything like put on subtitles or adjust the channel. Now a days we see remotes with little buttons. Most of the control buttons are now in the menu section where we can make adjustments there but not have them all over the place on the tv remote. As we look at the example of amazon, we can see clearly the big button to buy the product now. There are a few other buttons that are smaller that give options to save the product or search for other items for sale. This is a great example of Hick's Law to limit the amount of buttons and helps the consumer make a choice on whether or not they want to buy the product.
