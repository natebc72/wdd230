Demographics of Sahuarita

The population of Sahuarita is predominately white at almost 60%. This is contrasted with the big hispanic polulation coming in at 35% with other races taking the last 7%.

In Sahuarita you will find a large influence of the Spanish language due to the high hispanic population. Expect many different expressions of culture and cuisine!

In Sahuarita, 67% of the population lives in a married household which shows that Sahuarita is a family centered town. Best assured that families are a huge customer base for any growing business!

We placed above a graph depicting population in the year 2000 then jumped to 2010 and go up in two year increments to display the astounding growth that Sahuarita has gone under in 22 years. This gem of a town only shows growing promise as it shows close to an average growth of 2% a year. Businesses will flourish!

Sights of Sahuarita

Sahuarita is a town that lies adjacent to the community of Green Valley and is 15 miles south of Tucson, AZ. Sahuarita itself lies 50 miles north of the US-Mexico Border. Although it was officially recognized as a town in 1994, Sahuarita has been home to many for thousands of years.

Many local businesses reside along nationally known chain businesses within the many plazas in Sahuarita. The architecture reflects the South-West adobe style of acient indigineous people.

The Sahuarita Lake is a man-made lake which was opened in 2001. It has a perimeter of 1 mile and has a maximun depth of 10 feet. This lake provides the community with the opportunity of water recreation, fishing, and a relaxing and tranquil way to enjoy the amazing summer sunsets.

Madera Canyon is a part of the Coronado National Forest. It is located 20 miles southeast of Sahuarita and provides hiking, camping and sight-seeing for anyone looking to escape the heat of the desert. The highest point within the area of Madera Canyon is Mount Wrightson with an elevation of 9,456 feet above sea level.

The Greev Valley Pecan Groove is one of the world's largest growers of pecans with over 7000 acres of trees in Sahuarita, Green Valley, and San Simon. Operations were started in 1965 and continue to this day.
